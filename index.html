[ERR]rational

We are taught Mathematics as if it were a ruleset that governs all science, and logic. We treat it as a cold and unfeeling force that is best left to the 'rational thinkers', and it is this approach that keeps us, non-mathematicians, from appreciating the art and beauty within it.

Math is a rich and complicated language used to understand the world around us. A Sine wave can be used to describe the motion of our body as we walk, the creation of an oil painting can be understood through the lense of mathematics, and even music for many years was viewed as a field of mathematics.

The idea of rational vs. irrational, left brain vs right brain, or math vs. art is a barrier that we have placed upon ourselves that not only prevents us from experiencing the full creative potential of both math and art, but has also helped to develop gaps of inequality in the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM).

[ERR]rational uses Trigonometry to mathematically generate pieces of artwork to show the absurdity of the seperation between math and art.

So, what did we just experience?

In short we experienced mathematics. Since math is just a form of communication, this can be seen as a translation. Sin, Cos, and PI are not defined by mathematics. They are mere symbols of explaining relationships that exist within the world. [ERR]rational just uses shapes and sounds to express those relationships.


As I explain how this works, it is important to note that I am not a mathematician.
I am a creative technologist trying to express the unity between art and math. Many
of the decisions I have made are with artistic intent, and not with mathematical reasoning.

So, how was it made?

The audio and and visuals went through different 'translation' filters.

The Visuals:
Modeled using the Cinema 4D function spline. This allows you to draw a line following a certain equation for a number of "steps" or the variable t. For example the function Sin(t) with 4 steps. On the first step t = 1 means Sin(1) which is about .8, and then on the second step t = 2 and Sin(2) is about .9, and so on. In my models I used the equations seen above, with 44 steps.

The Audio:
For audio, the functions went through a more complex translation process than our 3d models. With the Tone.js library I loop my audio at 180 beats per minute. On each beat I calculate the value of the equation with "t" equaling time in seconds and use that value as a frequency that is then filtered through a synthesizer. The synthesizer is just a another calculation applied to that number to create something more akin to a musical note.

In Conclusion:
Each aspect of this experience has been mathematically generated. From a 3D environment that allows you to explore different sculptural pieces to the generated audio. This isn't to show that math isn't complicated, but instead express the richness and potential that it has. In class rooms we ask students to measure the distance of a trees shadow based on it's height, and they are left confused as to why will they need to know this information. We champion math as a pure, unfeeling and completely rational form of logic. Presenting math in this way dissuades those who are generalized as "irrational" from engaging in a field that may bring them joy. Our current approach to mathematics has robbed many from its beauty, and it is my hope that through this experience you too can see that we must bring creativity to the world of mathematics.
